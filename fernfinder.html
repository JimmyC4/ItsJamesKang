PROJECT GOALS

THE GIST:

The project goals for Fernfinder were founded on research on the user experience of novice plant owners, who had previously bought plants and had become discouraged after they died.

By researching other companies and websites, such as Bunnings, we gained insight into common conventions, techniques and necessities for a successful website.

PROJECT GOALS:

• Not only to sell, but to educate users on how to care for different plants
• Allow users to easily purchase and create an account
• Create a memorable experience through visuals, interactions and animations
• Motivate and bring confidence to our users as new plant owners

PERSONAS

UTILISING PERSONAS

Personas provide ficticious but representative insight into the wants, needs and frustrations of the users. Gathering this information helps guide me on what problems must be solved, what elements are essential to the user, client and website, and how I can best solve user wants, needs and fustrations.

Empathising for whom I am designing, allows me to design with the user in mind, instead of focusing too much on the product or service.

Here are some of the personas from this project:

MY TOOLS

Figma is the first tool I use when designing a website. Used for ideating, collating research, designing and even prototyping. Photoshop, AfterEffects and Illustrator are used for more complicated website elements.

Lastly for development, I used HTML, CSS and Javascript alongside React to build the interactive final design.

WARM PASTEL COLOUR PALETTE

COLOUR PALETTE

The warm, beige colours of Fernfinder are used to contrast against the natural green colours of the plants.

These colours also appealed to our target audience.

Our solution was to incorporate both the guide and shopping process of traditional plant selling websites, to create a one-stop shop where users can not only buy their plants, but learn to grow that specific plant with guides, tips and personalized quizzes.
